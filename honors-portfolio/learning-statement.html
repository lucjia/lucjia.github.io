Learning Statement

When I was admitted to UW and the Allen School in 2018, I thought that computer science (CS) was only about robots, hackathons, and big tech. I genuinely stumbled into this field by accident. However, during the department’s Early Fall Start (EFS) course, a lecture by Professor Emeritus Richard Ladner introduced me to how CS can be applied to advance equity and improve accessibility. Since then, I’ve embraced opportunities to connect my research and entrepreneurship experiences and drive social change with technology.

In my first year, I attended the Study Away Silicon Valley (SASV) program as one of five students in the UW delegation. This week cemented my interest in and passion for accessibility, answering questions while giving rise to new curiosities. Back then, I would have never guessed that this one week would lay the groundwork for me to start an accessible technology venture and reconsider my entire career trajectory.

Before my junior year, I never imagined pursuing academia because I had never seen anyone who looked like me in the field. Working with role models and mentors I could relate to was critical to inspiring me to break away from my fear of failure, to envision a career in academia, and to apply for a PhD. Inspired by the professors I had worked with, I came to the realization that I couldn't stop thinking about the projects that excited me so much more than the ones I had previously tackled in industry. In the fall of my senior year, I took a leap of faith and applied to PhD programs in HCI and accessibility. I'm excited to share that I will begin my graduate studies in computer science at Cornell University this fall!

I’m thankful for the extraordinary people and opportunities at UW that have helped me discover my future path and my passions for accessibility, teaching, and empathetic leadership. This journey has not been linear, nor has it been straightforward, but I believe that the obstacles and roadblocks that I faced have shaped me into who I am today. As I pursue a PhD and all of the possibilities that lie beyond, I aspire to become someone who can illuminate and empower others to find their own pathway.
